1. Scope of Information Collection Activities

In connection with the delivery of an effective and adaptive language learning experience, we engage in the collection of various categories of information. This collection occurs through multiple channels and serves multiple legitimate purposes related to service functionality and improvement.

1.1 Information Provided Directly by Users

Certain information is obtained directly from users during registration, profile creation, or other voluntary interactions. This may include account identifiers, contact details, demographic information, language learning objectives, payment processing details (handled through third-party providers), feedback submissions, support communications, and contributions to community features.

1.2 Audio and Interaction Data

The Service incorporates audio-based exercises that involve recording and analyzing spoken input. Such processing encompasses various acoustic elements and is necessary for providing feedback, assessing progress, and supporting underlying speech recognition and synthesis capabilities.

1.3 Technical and Usage Metadata

Various technical details are automatically collected to support operational requirements. These may include device identifiers, network information, session data, interaction patterns, and similar diagnostic or performance-related metrics.

1.4 Derived and Analytical Information

Additional insights may be generated through analytical processes applied to collected data. These derived elements contribute to personalization and ongoing service refinement.

The categories described above are illustrative rather than exhaustive, and collection practices may evolve in line with technological advancements and service developments.

The Service relies on sophisticated machine learning systems. Information collected from users contributes to the ongoing development and refinement of these systems through established training methodologies.

By using the Service, users grant a broad license permitting the incorporation of their interaction data into training processes. Once integrated into trained models, such information becomes part of the underlying architecture and may be retained accordingly.

These activities support continuous improvement of the platform and the broader advancement of language education technology.

Additional details regarding technical implementation may be available in supporting documentation or upon specific inquiry.
